# ArcGIS Online Guide for Beginners

ArcGIS Online is a powerful web-based GIS (Geographic Information System) tool that allows users to create, analyse, and share maps and spatial data. This guide provides a step-by-step process to get started with ArcGIS Online, from signing in to performing basic tasks such as map creation and data sharing.

## Introduction

ArcGIS Online is an online mapping platform developed by Esri. It provides tools for creating, visualising, and sharing interactive maps, as well as analysing spatial data. Whether you are a beginner or experienced GIS user, ArcGIS Online is an intuitive tool to work with maps and geospatial data in the cloud.

---

## Signing In to ArcGIS Online

Before you can use ArcGIS Online, you'll need to sign in. If you don’t have an account, you can sign up for a free public account or obtain a subscription from your organisation.

### Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Go to the ArcGIS Online Website**:
   - Open your browser and go to [https://www.arcgis.com](https://www.arcgis.com).

2. **Click "Sign In"**:
   - On the top-right corner of the website, click on the **Sign In** button.

3. **Sign In Options**:
   - If you have an ArcGIS account, enter your **username** and **password**.
   - If you don’t have an account, click **Create an account** to set one up.

### Sign In Using Organizational URL

If your organisation has a subscription to ArcGIS Online, you can sign in using your organisation's URL. This will give you access to organisation-specific content, tools, and shared resources.

1. **Choose "Sign in with your ArcGIS organization"**:
   - After clicking **Sign In**, you will be prompted to either sign in with your Esri account or your organization's account. Choose the **Sign in with your ArcGIS organization** option.

2. **Enter Your Organization's URL**:
   - You will be asked to enter your organisation’s ArcGIS Online URL. This typically looks like: `https://<your_organisation_name>.maps.arcgis.com` (https://geouow.maps.arcgis.com/).

3. **Enter Your Credentials (using SSO account)**:
   - After entering your organisation's URL, you will be prompted to enter your organisation's **username** and **password**.

4. **Access Your Dashboard**:
   - Once logged in, you’ll be taken to your **Content** page, which acts as a dashboard for managing your maps, data, and other resources within your organisation.

## Navigating the ArcGIS Online Interface

The ArcGIS Online interface is user-friendly and allows for easy navigation. Here's an overview of the key components you'll be using:

1. **Header**: 
   - Contains the ArcGIS logo, search bar, and your user profile.
2. **Menu Bar**: 
   - Provides access to your content, groups, maps, apps, and other resources.
3. **Content**: 
   - Displays your maps, layers, and apps. This is the default page when you log in.
4. **Map Viewer**: 
   - This is the tool you'll use to create and edit your maps. You can access the map viewer from the menu bar.
5. **Basemap Gallery**: 
   - Includes different basemaps (e.g., imagery, topographic, street maps) that you can use to build your maps.

## Creating a Map

Once you're signed in and familiar with the interface, it’s time to create a new map.

### Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Go to the Map Viewer**:
   - From the ArcGIS Online home page, click on **Map** in the top menu bar to open the **Map Viewer**.

2. **Choose a Basemap**:
   - Once the map viewer opens, click the **Basemap** button on the right to choose a base layer (e.g., streets, imagery, terrain, etc.).

3. **Add Layers**:
   - Click on **Add** > **Search for Layers** to search for existing layers from ArcGIS Online or your own data.

4. **Zoom and Navigate**:
   - Use the zoom tools to focus on the area you want to map.
   - You can click and drag the map to move it.

5. **Save the Map**:
   - Once you're happy with your map, click **Save** in the top left corner.
   - Enter a title, tags, and a description for your map.

## Adding Data to Your Map

Adding data to your map can help you visualise geographic features, such as roads, rivers, cities, or other relevant spatial information.

### Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Search for Layers**:
   - Click **Add** > **Search for Layers** to search for layers in ArcGIS Online or your own content.

2. **Add Data from Your Computer**:
   - If you have data stored on your computer (e.g., Supported file types are CSV, GeoJSON, KML, GeoPackage), click **Add** > **Add Layer from File** and upload the data.

3. **Add Web Layers**:
   - You can also add web layers (e.g., WMS, KML, or Feature Services) by selecting **Add** > **Add Layer from Web**.

4. **Configure Layer Properties**:
   - Once layers are added, click on the layer in the **Contents** pane to open its properties. You can change the style, visibility, and pop-ups.

---

## Saving Your Map

Once your map is created and data has been added, you may want to share it with others or save it for future use.

### Step-by-Step Guide

**Save Your Map**:
   - Click the **Save** button in the top-left corner.
   - Add a title, description, and tags to your map, then click **Save**.
