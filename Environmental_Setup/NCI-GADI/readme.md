## About NCI

The National Computational Infrastructure (NCI) is Australia's premier provider of high-performance computing, data storage, and data analysis services. NCI provides researchers with cutting-edge resources and support to perform complex simulations, data analysis, and computational tasks across a wide range of scientific disciplines.

NCI is designed to support research across diverse fields such as climate science, biology, engineering, and physics. By leveraging powerful computational resources like Gadi, NCI enables researchers to tackle large-scale computational challenges and accelerate discovery.

You can learn more about NCI at [NCI Website](https://nci.org.au).

---

## About Gadi

Gadi is one of the most powerful supercomputers available in Australia and is part of the National Computational Infrastructure (NCI). Gadi is specifically designed to support a wide range of scientific and engineering tasks, providing the computing power required to solve some of the most complex problems faced by researchers today.

With over 1.1 million CPU cores, Gadi offers high-performance computing to support everything from large-scale simulations to machine learning applications. The system is used by thousands of researchers across multiple disciplines, offering both computational resources and high-speed storage solutions.

Learn more about Gadi at [Gadi Information Page](https://nci.org.au/systems/gadi).
